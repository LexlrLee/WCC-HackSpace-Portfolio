Harmonic Fluidity: The Art of Soundscapes and Motion

Zhengyang Li

An interactive audiovisual installation immersing visitors in a fluid world of particles and sound, revealing the interplay between auditory influences and the invisible forces shaping our environment.

Introduction

My aim instead was to make an interactive audiovisual installation that explores the relationship between sound and the movement of particles in a fluid-like environment. This artwork encourages visitors to consider the role of sound in shaping the environment and the invisible forces that govern the natural world. By interacting with the artwork, visitors become part of a symphony, giving expression to the constant flow of particles and music.

Concept and Background Research

Since the late nineteenth century, the advent and popularity of video technology and the medium of film and television have created a real-time, reflective sonic stimulus for the viewer, demonstrating an astonishing creativity that has made video more epic and enjoyable and has gradually opened a whole new way of seeing and experiencing the world.

My purpose in making this audio-visual interactive work is to create an immersive atmosphere that evokes a sense of calm and serenity. When the viewer enters the field of this work it evokes a sense of peace and tranquillity. I want as many young people as possible to slow down from the city's fast-paced life and feel the present moment.
